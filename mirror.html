Their Impressions

~300 Word Summary

When I looked back at the feedback from my two interviewees‚Äîmy childhood friend (A) and my college peer (B)‚ÄîI realized just how big the gap is between the version of me people see and the version of me I actually live with.

They said they've always seen how driven I am, but they also know that a lot of that drive comes from my anxiety. This really confirmed my belief that I need to slowly close the gap between those two sides of myself, so that the people around me can understand me more fully, not just the part of me that looks strong and organized on the outside.

Bridging the Gap

Hearing how differently people see me made me realize how much of myself I keep tucked away. My childhood friend sees the real, messy, anxious parts of me‚Äîthe parts I try to hide‚Äîwhile my college peer mostly sees the organized, reliable version I show in public. Both views are true, but only one feels honest. The one thing they both noticed, though, is my dedication, and that reminded me that despite my inner struggles, I genuinely give my best to the people and things I care about. This whole experience made me aware of how wide the gap is between my inner and outer self, and it pushes me to start being more open about what I feel, not just what I accomplish.
